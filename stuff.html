About ImTheBus

ImTheBus is a small collection of practical tools that live on a shared whiteboard. They exist to help managers and teams think more clearly about work, decisions, and how people operate together. The goal is simple: fewer vague conversations, more useful ones.

Why this site exists

The tools on this site are built around a few simple ideas:

  • Make fuzzy topics feel concrete enough to talk about.
  • Give teams a neutral place to put thoughts and decisions.
  • Encourage experiments instead of grand plans.
  • Help people bring others with them, not leave them behind.

ImTheBus is not a polished product. It is a working bench of prototypes, frameworks, and little experiments that might be useful in real organisations.

Terms of use

This site is offered as is, with no guarantees. In plain language:

  • Use the tools at your own risk and judgement.
  • The tools do not replace legal, HR, medical, or financial advice.
  • You are responsible for how you apply anything from this site in your workplace.
  • The site may change, break, or disappear without notice.

Privacy and data

The default assumption is that your data belongs to you.

  • The tools are designed to run in your browser with minimal data collection.
  • Any future sign in or team features will be clearly explained and optional.
  • Analytics, if used, will focus on broad usage, not tracking individuals.
  • If you type confidential information into any tool, treat it as you would any other web form.

If a more formal privacy notice is added later, it will be linked clearly from this page.

Using this whiteboard well

These tools work best when you:

  • Share the screen and draw together, rather than filling it in alone.
  • Write in simple language that everyone in the room understands.
  • Treat everything as a draft that can be revisited or thrown away.
  • Capture outcomes somewhere sensible afterwards, such as your own notes or systems.

The About page is just one more sticky on the board. If something here does not work for you, you are encouraged to adapt it.
