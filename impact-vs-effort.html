Impact vs Effort
Use an Impact vs Effort grid to spot quick wins, big bets, low-value fillers, and work to challenge.

This layout comes from the classic Impact vs Effort matrix. It helps you compare how much a task could move the dial against how much time, energy, and coordination it really needs.

High impact and low effort tasks show up as quick wins. High impact and high effort tasks become big bets that you plan and support properly.

Low impact work with low effort sits in filler territory, and low impact work with high effort is where you challenge, redesign, delegate, or drop the task entirely.

Add tasks on the left, answer the questions to place each one on the board, then work from the priority list at the bottom. Quick wins come first, then the right big bets, followed by fillers and tasks to avoid or question.
