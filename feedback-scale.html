Feedback scale
A quick way to map how feedback feels at the moment across five zones: Silent, Sparse, Resonant, Loud and Overwhelming.
Use this with a colleague or manager to get a shared picture of how feedback is landing, then agree one or two experiments to improve it.

This tool turns gut feelings about feedback into a simple picture you can look at with your manager or team. It does not judge whether feedback is good or bad. Instead it shows where the weight sits: in silence, in useful conversation, or in noisy or overwhelming feedback.

Answer the questions based on how feedback has felt over the last few days or weeks, then use the results to have a constructive conversation about what would make feedback more useful and sustainable for you.

It is often safest to talk through the results together and treat them as a shared problem to solve, not a scorecard on any one person.
